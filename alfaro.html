Exploring Computational Biology

Unveiling evolutional relationships through computational tools.

Position Description:

Duration: Jul 2021 - Jun 2022

The UCLA Alfaro Lab (Ecology and Evolutionary Biology department) studies macro evolution and biodiversity through the usage of computational biology. I worked at the Alfaro Lab as a research assistant on various computational biology projects, in which I employed R, C++, and other quantitative tools to perform animal pattern, genetic, and evolutional analysis.

Fish Tree of Life Project

> In this project, I worked on optimizing the daily genetic data integration process for the largest-ever time-calibrated tree of ray-finned fishes, increasing runtime efficiency for the Fish Tree of Life R package and website.

> Languages Used: Python, R

Color Pattern Analysis

> In this project, I developed code to generate various color pattern images with specified color ratios and random distributions, examples of which can be seen here to the left.

> These images served as testing input data for the R tool Charisma, which the Alfaro Lab developed to analyze color patterns in birds and fish.

> Languages Used: R

Population genetics simulation

> In this project, I worked with two other students to generate simulations of population genetics dynamics to evaluate the impact of varying ecological conditions on squirrel fitness levels. .

> I presented this research project at the 2021 annual UCLA Biology Research Symposium.

> Read more about this project here: Presentation Link

> Languages Used: C++

Eel Evolutionary trees

> In this project, I was given evolutionary data in the form of CSV files.

> Employing the ggtree R package, I generated large moray eel phylogenetic trees and programmed eel visualizations onto the tree.

> Technologies Used: R, ggtree
